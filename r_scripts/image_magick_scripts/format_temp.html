Determine if a Tweet is about elitism or arrogance at a university.

Overview

In this task, you will determine if a Tweet or the content of any accompanying links are related to the perceived elitism or arrogance at a university. Your work will make a major contribution toward understanding on line conversations about universities.

For this job, you will judge tweets about "Stanford University" or "Stanford," a research and teaching university located in California.

We Provide

  • Author of post
  • Piece of content (a Tweet)
  • A link to view the content as it appears on Twitter

Process

  1. Read the Tweet.

  2. If there is not a Tweet pictured, click on the link to view the Tweet.

  3. Visit the links found in the text for additional context.
    • Click on links in the Tweet to see the post on Twitter.
    • You don't need to read the entire web page, but read its headline and first few sentences to determine whether it is about elitism or arrogance at Stanford.

  4. Determine whether the tweet is related to elitism or arrogance at Stanford University.

  5. If the Tweet is about elitism, determine if the sentiment of the author was positive, neutral, or negative.

Examples and Tips


DO apply the following rules when classifying sentiment

  • Negative
    • Tweets that describe Stanford or people at Stanford as: stuck up, elitist, pretentious, pompous, highbrow, haughty, smug or vain.

      • "I can't stand #Stanford bunch of stuck up cry babies.... you lost Stanford"

      • "Why the hell is it the #Stanford #cardinal was there only one cardinal? Quit being pretentious and put an 's' at the end Of your name #dumb"

  • Neutral
    • The tweet does not contain a positive or negative sentiment regarding elitism or arrogance

  • Positive
    • A positive sentiment regarding elitism or arrogance will be rare

      • "The Stanford Cardinal has to be the most brilliantly highbrow mascot ever"

DO NOT judge the following posts to be about elitism or arrogance at Stanford

  • Tweets that don't have anything to do with elitism or arrogance at Stanford university.

    • "The Duck fans aren't going to know who to pompously brag to after this epic beat down by #Stanford. For that, I am thankful."

  • Tweets where the author, not Stanford, is bragging about Stanford.

    • "@ButcherBoy415 @Stanford That graduation rate is something to brag about. What's Florida's graduation rate?"

  • A Tweet may mention the word 'elite' or 'elitist.' Read the post carefully to determine if Stanford is being negatively judged as elite or elitist.

    • "Found a new reason to be a #Colts fan... @CobyFleener not only an elite athlete but got that #Stanford intelligence #dreamy #yesplease"

  • These cases should all be answered "No, this post is not about elitism or arrogance at Stanford"
